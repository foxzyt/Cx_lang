## Decisions and direction

The compound assign lowering follows the established SSA pattern already used for plain assignment: read current binding value, compute result, emit new `SsaBind`, update binding map. The choice to reject `DotAccess` explicitly (rather than silently falling through) is consistent with how the lowering pass handles other unsupported constructs — it returns a typed error rather than panicking. This keeps the backend honest about what it can and cannot lower.
