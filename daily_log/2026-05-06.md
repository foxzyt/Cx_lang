## Decisions and direction

**Phase 14 is real.** The Cranelift JIT backend went from stub to executing actual Cx programs in one day. CX-25 and CX-26 prove the pipeline end-to-end: ConstInt, Binary arithmetic, Return, Alloca, Load, Store all compile and execute correctly. CX-41 added Jump and Branch, bringing the total to 191 passing JIT tests.

**Runtime intrinsics boundary is taking shape.** CX-35 audited all builtins and documented the boundary spec. CX-38 (in review) implements the actual dispatch mechanism: `printn` routes through a two-pass compilation model where intrinsics are declared as Cranelift imports and linked to extern "C" shims. This is the first Cx code that can produce visible output through the JIT path.

**Loop-variable safety landed.** CX-40's read-only enforcement in the IR validator means the `for i in range` counter cannot be reassigned inside the loop body — the invariant is enforced at the IR level, not just semantically.

**Submain → main gap cycle.** Main was synced via PR #76 early in the day, but submain immediately diverged again with 8 new non-merge commits (CX-25 through CX-41). The gap is structural: submain serves as integration staging, main as the stable tip.
